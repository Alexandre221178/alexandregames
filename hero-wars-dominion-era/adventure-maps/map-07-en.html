Ghirwil City Depths - Map #7

  • Minimum Hero Level: 80+
  • Recommended Total Power: 90,000 to 124,000

This adventure requires solid coordination among three guild members. Each player should choose a specific path (Blue, Yellow, or Green) and wait at buff points before proceeding to remove tower buffs. The more towers your team defeats, the weaker the enemy squads become including the boss. Team synergy and strategic movement are essential for success.

How to Defeat the Boss

The final boss is extremely powerful due to multiple buffs:

  • +100% damage dealt
  • -75% damage taken
  • 10x more Health
  • +100% energy gain
  • -80% debuff duration
  • No skill can deal more than 1% of its max HP
  • Immune to energy reduction

These boosts make the boss nearly invincible unless your team removes key buffs first. Thatâ€™s why defeating specific Eye of Harbinger squads is essential:

  • Squad 24: Removes -75% damage taken buff.
  • Squad 25: Removes another -75% damage taken buff.
  • Squad 26: Removes the +160% damage dealt buff.

Coordinate with your teammates to clear these squads before engaging the boss. If skipped, the boss will retain critical resistances, significantly increasing your chances of defeat.

Additional Tip: Squad 10

While not part of the mandatory boss debuff path, defeating Squad 10 also removes a Damage Resist buff that decreases the boss's damage taken by 50%.

If your team has time and action points to spare, it's strongly recommended to clear Squad 10. This further reduces the bossâ€™s durability and improves your chances of success.

Conclusion

Ghirwil City Depths is a challenging Adventure requiring strategy, patience, and perfect timing. Stick to your path, take out tower and squad buffs in the right order, and cooperate with your team. Remember, the boss is tough but with proper coordination and pathing, your guild will triumph and earn top-tier pet rewards!

Always communicate with your guildmates, plan your routes ahead of time, and don't rush into buffed enemies. Smart teamwork always beats brute force.
