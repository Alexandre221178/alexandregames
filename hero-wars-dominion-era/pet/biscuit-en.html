Biscuit is a control-type pet that plays from the back line in Hero Wars: Dominion Era. Known for its high magic damage and strength, Biscuit is excellent for weakening enemy healing and supporting your team from afar.

In this guide, you’ll discover Biscuit’s maximum stats, key strengths, and how to effectively deploy this pet in battle. Whether you're optimizing your team or just starting out, Biscuit is a reliable support asset to consider.

Who Is Biscuit?

Biscuit is a powerful control pet in Hero Wars: Dominion Era. With high magic penetration and strength stats, Biscuit disrupts enemy abilities while enhancing your team’s survivability. Ideal for teams that rely on backline support, Biscuit can turn the tide of battle by interrupting enemy skills.

Biscuit Skills Upgrade Priority - Hero Wars: Dominion Era

Biscuit reduces healing and controls enemies with icy attacks. Perfect pet for shutting down enemy sustain and support units.

Biscuit Active Skills

Icy Storm

This is Biscuit’s first and main active skill. When used, Biscuit summons a storm under the enemies that deals magic damage and stops them from being healed by allies for 5 seconds. This is very useful when fighting enemies with strong healers, like Martha or Celeste, because it blocks their team’s healing effects temporarily.

Mighty Snowball

This is Biscuit’s second active skill, which unlocks when he reaches green rank. Biscuit throws a magic snowball at the first enemy trying to heal. It deals magic damage and slows them down. The snowball takes 10 seconds to prepare, so it won’t activate right away in battle. It's a helpful skill to stop enemies who try to heal quickly or escape.

Biscuit Patronage Skill

Stayin’ Chill

This is Biscuit’s patronage skill, unlocked when he reaches violet rank. When Biscuit is assigned to a hero, that hero’s attacks reduce the healing the enemy receives by 30% for 5 seconds. This is great for long fights or versus healing-based teams. Even if Biscuit isn’t on the battlefield, this passive effect still works!

Biscuit Pros and Cons - Hero Wars: Web and Facebook

✅ Pros

  • Excellent anti-healing tool against heal-heavy teams like Celeste, Maya, or Dorian teams.
  • Reduces both healing from enemy team skills and item-based effects.
  • Great synergy with control heroes who stall fights, maximizing healing reduction uptime.
  • Works well even off-field via Patronage strong healing debuff effect without being in battle.
  • Good magic penetration scaling with high Skill Power for increased damage output.

❌ Cons

  • Not effective against teams that don't rely on healing.
  • Slow activation: the snowball skill has a 10-second prep time before use.
  • Low burst damage compared to offensive pets like Axel or Fenris.
  • Does not offer protection or energy control focuses solely on healing disruption.
  • Requires investment to violet rank to unlock full Patronage skill potential.

Conclusion - Is Biscuit Worth It in Hero Wars: Dominion Era?

Biscuit stands out as a strong counter to healing-based teams in Hero Wars: Dominion Era. With both active and patronage abilities focused on reducing enemy healing, it plays a unique role that no other pet fully replaces. Its Icy Storm and healing reduction synergy make it extremely effective against heroes like Celeste, Maya, or Thea, especially in long battles.

However, Biscuit is not a universal choice. Against teams that rely more on burst damage or crowd control rather than healing, Biscuit’s utility drops. It also lacks offensive or defensive boosts like Axel or Cain, and its snowball ability takes time to activate, which can reduce its effectiveness in fast-paced fights.

Still, for players looking to build a versatile team capable of shutting down sustain-based enemies, Biscuit is a valuable investment. It excels in PvP and boss fights where healing reduction can tilt the battle. With the right synergy and proper upgrades, Biscuit can become an essential tool in your Dominion lineup.
