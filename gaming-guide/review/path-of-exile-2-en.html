The Third Edict update brought a bunch of class balancing tweaks, which means the meta has changed a lot since the last patch. Below, I’m going to show how some of the top Ascendancies have been knocked off the leaderboard, while others have risen to replace them.

This PoE 2 Ascendancy Tier List will highlight the best and worst classes following the Third Edict Update. Ready to dive into the action? Grab some PoE 2 Currency, like Chaos Orbs or Divine Orbs, and prepare to face Wraeclast.

Path of Exile 2 Ascendancy Class Tier List

S Tier

  • Deadeye
  • Blood Mage
  • Infernalist

Deadeye

Deadeye’s upgrades to its projectile system and the new Avidity passive have helped it secure the top spot in this tier list. This build now boasts some of the highest single-target damage with Ascendancy, along with great mob-clearing skills, making Deadeye a solid choice for both league starter and end-game.

Blood Mage

Although it’s a bit trickier as a league starter, this Ascendancy quickly rises once you unlock more infusion-based spells. Mana-casting enhancements have improved keystones like Black Flame and Life Overflow, making this build even more powerful.

Infernalist

Currently the strongest minion Ascendancy in Path of Exile 2, Infernalist has fantastic fire infusion generation and impressive late-game critical strike scaling. A great pick for both league starter and end-game.

A Tier

  • Lich
  • Witchhunter
  • Tactician
  • Amazon

Lich

The Lich Ascendancy is the toughest among the minion classes. Great mob-clearing abilities and strong damage scaling keep it high, but Infernalist out-damages it, keeping Lich in A tier.

Witchhunter

Popular for both league starters and end-game due to Weapon Set passives and improved weapon-swapping mechanics. Strength-focused with strong curse and poison synergies, but needs more investment to match S-tier damage.

Tactician

Tacticians are fast and utility-rich, with banners and buffs that boost totems and minions. Their reliance on glory generation and banner uptime limits their placement.

Amazon

Solid overall, but Deadeye outperforms it in many areas. Nerfs to critical strikes have reduced its effectiveness; accuracy and stat investment are needed to compete.

B Tier

  • Stormweaver
  • Pathfinder
  • Smith of Kitava
  • Warbringer

Stormweaver

Received many buffs and QoL improvements, but still struggles with infusion generation. Some key abilities no longer generate infusion, reducing consistent damage potential.

Pathfinder

Reliable DPS through poison, flask synergy, and chaos DoTs. Solid but underwhelming compared to higher-tier ascendancies.

Smith of Kitava

Excellent starter ascendancy with strong survivability and clear speed. It falls off in end-game DPS scaling.

Warbringer

Great improvements to its core skills boosted the kit, but it requires substantial gear investment to match higher tiers, placing it in B tier.

C Tier

  • Chronomancer
  • Acolyte of Chayula
  • Invoker
  • Titan

Chronomancer

Carved a niche with curse scaling and detonation mechanics. Initial damage can feel slow, but late-game DPS remains solid.

Acolyte of Chayula

Tends to depend on specific gems like Volatility for consistent damage. Decent to play but other classes achieve more with less hassle.

Invoker

Tricky for newer players due to mana and cooldown challenges. Useful for hardcore or end-game, but recent nerfs to spells like Frost Bomb have hurt performance.

Titan

Solid league starter thanks to Hulking Form, but it doesn’t scale well for end-game damage and lacks ranged options, making late-game content tougher.

D Tier

  • Gemling Legionnaire
  • Ritualist

Gemling Legionnaire

Hit hard by recent nerfs and changes to charge generation. Embedding high-tier gems into armor has reduced its effectiveness.

Ritualist

Not the best league starter because it relies heavily on good gear. Can still shine in end-game with top-notch items, but overall riskier for new players.

Players should keep in mind that the game is still in Early Access and the meta is unstable. Small changes can greatly affect ascendancies, so this tier list is subject to change at any moment.

Conclusion

The Third Edict update has reshaped the Path of Exile 2 meta, shaking up the top Ascendancies and creating new opportunities for players to explore. While S-Tier classes like Deadeye, Blood Mage, and Infernalist remain dominant choices, many A and B-Tier Ascendancies can still shine with the right gear, strategy, and playstyle.

Ultimately, the best class for you depends on whether you want a strong league starter, reliable end-game performance, or a unique play experience. Since Path of Exile 2 is still evolving in Early Access, keep an eye out for future patches that may completely shift this tier list. Choose wisely, experiment often, and most importantly enjoy the journey through Wraeclast.
