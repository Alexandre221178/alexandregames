Complete Guide to Playing Minotaur in Mobile Legends(MLBB)

Minotaur is a powerful tank in Mobile Legends: Bang Bang, known for his ability to initiate fights, control enemies, and provide healing and protection to his teammates. His unique rage mechanic sets him apart from other heroes, as he transforms into an enraged state, significantly enhancing his abilities. In this guide, we'll dive deep into how to maximize Minotaur's potential, breaking down each of his skills, the best combos, item builds, emblem choices, and strategies for early, mid, and late-game phases.

Minotaur's Skill Breakdown and Usage

Passive: Rage Incarnate

Effect: When Minotaur applies a control effect to an enemy hero with a skill, he will also reduce their Hybrid Defense by 5 (+0.5*Hero Level). When Minotaur heals an allied hero with a skill, he will also increase their Hybrid Defense by 5 (+0.5*Hero Level) for 2s. These effects are doubled in Enraged state.

Usage Tip: Take advantage of Minotaur's Rage Incarnate by carefully timing your control skills and heals during fights. When you're about to engage or support, aim to trigger your crowd control abilities (like knock-ups) on high-priority enemy targets, reducing their Hybrid Defense and making them more vulnerable to your team's attacks. Similarly, use your healing abilities strategically in the middle of a team fight to boost your allies' Hybrid Defense. If you're close to entering your Enraged State, wait for it before engaging, as the passive effects are doubled, significantly amplifying both your offensive and defensive impact.

Skill 1: Despair Stomp

Cooldown: 12 seconds

Effect: Minotaur jumps to a target location, dealing AoE physical damage and briefly knocking enemies airborne. It also slows enemies by 40% and enhances his basic attacks for 2 seconds.

Enraged Version: The area of effect and damage are significantly increased.

Usage Tip: Despair Stomp is your primary engage and crowd control tool. In its enraged form, it covers a much larger area, so always aim to use it when enraged to maximize its impact. You can use it to disrupt enemy formations or to peel for your carries by knocking up enemies who try to dive on them.

Skill 2: Motivation Roar

Cooldown: 10 seconds

Effect: Minotaur heals himself and nearby allied heroes, recovering a percentage of his lost HP.

Enraged Version: When enraged, Minotaur gains Enraged Regen, allowing him to recover HP equal to 100% of the damage taken for 2 seconds.

Usage Tip: Motivation Roar is your sustain tool in fights, and the healing is invaluable for both you and your team. Use this skill strategically in team fights—try to pop it when multiple allies need healing, or when you’re about to take a lot of damage in your enraged state to maximize its effectiveness. In combination with healing items like Oracle, this ability becomes even more potent.

Ultimate: Minoan Fury

Cooldown: 60 seconds

Effect: Minotaur enters his Enraged State and smashes the ground three times, dealing physical damage and applying slows and knock-ups. The final hit deals True Damage.

Usage Tip: Minoan Fury is one of the best teamfight ultimates in the game due to its wide-area crowd control. Save it for key moments when you can hit multiple enemies, especially the enemy backline or squishy carries. Always be mindful of enemy crowd control that could interrupt your ultimate if you’re not in your enraged form.

Minotaur Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Excellent Crowd Control: Multiple skills with knock-ups, slows, and a devastating area-of-effect ultimate make Minotaur one of the best crowd control tanks in the game.
  • High Sustain: His healing ability can keep both himself and his allies alive longer during team fights.
  • Tanky with Good Scaling: Minotaur becomes incredibly difficult to kill in the late game, especially with proper itemization, thanks to his healing and rage mechanics.
  • Great Team Fight Presence: In his enraged state, Minotaur can heavily influence the outcome of team fights, often being the difference between victory and defeat.

Cons:

  • Dependent on Rage: Minotaur's effectiveness significantly drops when he is not enraged, so managing his Rage is key.
  • Vulnerable Early Game: Lacks strong crowd control or tankiness before building items and gaining levels.
  • Skillshot Reliant: His jump and ultimate are skillshots that require good positioning and timing to hit effectively.
  • Long Cooldowns: Especially early in the game, Minotaur’s cooldowns can feel long, making him less effective in quick skirmishes.

Minotaur Combos

Combo 1: Skill 1 -> Basic Attack -> Skill 3 (Enhanced Minoan Fury) -> Skill 2 -> Basic Attack

Execution: Engage with Despair Stomp to knock up and slow the enemies. Follow up with a basic attack before launching into Minoan Fury to disrupt the enemy team and deal massive damage. Finish with Motivation Roar for healing and sustain, and another basic attack to keep up the pressure.

Combo 2: Despair Stomp -> Basic Attack -> Minoan Fury -> Motivation Roar -> Basic Attack

Execution: A more aggressive version of the first combo, focusing on maximizing your AoE damage. Use Despair Stomp to initiate, then immediately follow with Minoan Fury to keep the enemy crowd controlled. Finish with Motivation Roar to keep yourself and your allies alive while continuing to deal damage with basic attacks.

Flicker Combo Tip:

Usage Tip: Flicker can be used in combination with your first skill or ultimate to catch enemies off guard. Use Flicker to position yourself for an optimal Minoan Fury or to escape dangerous situations after a fight.

How to Counter Minotaur in Mobile Legends

Counters What Happens

Carmilla
Carmilla can't stop Minotaur's ultimate, but she can turn it around if the enemy rushes in, making her a great counter in team fights.

Minsitthar
Minsitthar prevents Minotaur from jumping, ensuring he won’t knock up enemies twice, reducing his effectiveness in fights.

Franco
Franco can shut down Minotaur's ultimate with his hook and ultimate, making him a powerful counter.

Lolita
Lolita’s ultimate slows down Minotaur, making it harder for him to engage effectively in team fights.

Mathilda
Mathilda becomes immune to Minotaur's abilities when she is in the air, making her difficult for him to control during engagements.

X.Borg
X.Borg becomes immune to Minotaur's abilities when his Firaga Armor is active, allowing him to fight without worrying about crowd control.

Diggie
Diggie's ultimate can free allies from Minotaur's crowd control, making him an excellent support counter in team battles.

Minotaur Global Best Builds

1. Tough Boots - Favor

Stats: +40 Movement Speed, +18 Magic Defense

Passive - Devotion: Helps you tank objectives early by forgoing gold and experience for the sake of your team.

Passive - Blessing: Once you've accumulated enough gold, you gain access to additional healing effects.

Reasoning: This item gives you magic defense and movement speed, while the passive helps your healing abilities.

2. Flask of the Oasis

Stats: +60 Magic Power, +300 HP, +10% Cooldown Reduction

Passive: Heals allies and provides a shield when they fall below 35% HP, with cooldown reduction when triggered.

Reasoning: Excellent for providing additional sustain to your team and enhances your healing potential, especially in mid-to-late game team fights.

3. Oracle

Stats: +850 HP, +20 Physical Defense, +20 Magic Defense, +10% Cooldown Reduction

Passive - Bless: Increases shield and healing effects by 30%.

Reasoning: Perfect for enhancing Minotaur’s healing ability from his second skill, making him even harder to kill.

4. Winter Crown

Stats: +45 Adaptive Attack, +400 HP, +5% Cooldown Reduction

Active Skill - Frozen: Temporarily immune to damage and untargetable for 2 seconds.

Reasoning: A powerful defensive item that allows you to survive dangerous situations and avoid being burst down.

5. Athena's Shield or Radiant Armor

Stats: +900 HP, +20 Magic Defense, +10% Cooldown Reduction

Passive: Blocks the first skill or basic attack that deals more than 200 magic damage.

Reasoning: Choose between Athena's Shield for magic defense and extra HP, or Radiant Armor to reduce incoming magic damage over time, depending on the enemy team composition.

6. Immortality

Stats: +800 HP, +40 Physical Defense

Passive: Revives you after death with a portion of your HP.

Reasoning: Best used in the late game when respawn times are long, giving you a second chance to survive crucial fights.

Minotaur Gameplay Strategy

Early Game

Play Passive: Minotaur's early game isn’t particularly strong, so focus on supporting your jungler and mage by tanking objectives. Provide map vision and assist in rotations rather than initiating team fights, as your power spikes once you reach level 4 and unlock your ultimate.

Roam and Rotate: Once your jungler is finished clearing camps, rotate to help the gold lane or top lane. Prioritize gaining vision and controlling the map rather than forcing fights.

Mid Game

Initiate with Precision: Use the bushes to surprise enemies with Despair Stomp, followed by your ultimate to initiate team fights. Focus on disrupting the enemy backline and protecting your carries.

Sustain in Fights: Use Motivation Roar frequently to heal your team during skirmishes. Your healing will allow you and your allies to stay in fights longer, forcing the enemy to overextend or retreat.

Late Game

Be the Frontline: Minotaur shines in the late game. Always be at the forefront of team fights, initiating with your Minoan Fury and using Despair Stomp to disrupt the enemy’s formation. Your durability and healing will make it hard for enemies to burst you down, allowing you to create space for your damage dealers.

Team Fight Initiation: Always look to be the one starting team fights. Your combination of crowd control and healing makes you a crucial component of your team’s success in late-game engagements.

Minotaur Guide Conclusion

Minotaur is a formidable tank that can make a massive impact on the battlefield with his crowd control and healing abilities. Mastering his Rage mechanic and understanding when to engage and disengage is key to unlocking his full potential. With the right build, emblems, and gameplay strategy, Minotaur can dominate team fights and help lead his team to victory. Good luck on the battlefield, and may your roars strike fear into the hearts of your enemies!
