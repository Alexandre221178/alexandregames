Detailed Tips for Each Reworked Skill

1. Qing Long (Gray Skill)

Description: Qing Mao summons a dragon spirit that strikes three nearby enemies, dealing physical damage with each hit. Each strike sets enemies on fire for 5 seconds, pushes them back, and inflicts burning damage over time.

  • Damage per strike: 35% Physical Attack + 75 × Level
  • Burning damage per second: Physical Attack + 75 × Level

Battle Impact:

This skill is a powerhouse for dealing area damage and controlling enemy movement. The combination of burst and burn damage ensures that enemies take continuous punishment, making it particularly effective against clusters of enemies. Use this skill to weaken frontline Tanks and pressure fragile backline heroes simultaneously. Additionally, the pushback adds crowd control, giving your team extra breathing room to reposition or launch follow-up attacks.

2. Spear of Dawn (Green Skill)

Description: Qing Mao blinds the nearest enemies for 4 seconds, causing their attacks to miss, and deals physical damage.

  • Damage: 40% Physical Attack + 30 × Level

Battle Impact:

This skill is powerful for dealing area damage and controlling enemy movement. The combination of immediate damage and continuous burning ensures that enemies suffer constant punishment, making it particularly effective against groups of enemies. This ability weakens frontline Tanks and simultaneously pressures fragile backline heroes. Additionally, the pushback adds crowd control, giving your team more room to reposition or launch follow-up attacks.

3. Dragon’s Claw (Blue Skill)

Description: Qing Mao deals physical damage to the nearest enemy based on their current health. This skill is particularly effective against high-health targets, as its damage scales with the enemy’s health pool.

  • Damage: 20% of the target’s current health

Battle Impact:

This ability makes Qing Mao a natural Tank killer. Against high-health enemies, such as Astaroth or Rufus, Qing Mao can strip their health significantly at the start of a fight, breaking down their defenses quickly. This skill ensures that enemy Tanks are no longer an insurmountable obstacle, opening a clear path for your team to target backline damage dealers and supports.

4. Open Heart (Purple Skill)

Description: Every time Qing Mao strikes with a physical attack, she steals a portion of the target’s armor, weakening their defense. For every Way of Honor ally in the team, the armor steal effect is amplified. The stolen armor remains for the rest of the battle or until Qing Mao is defeated.

  • Armor steal per strike: 1.5% Physical Attack + 5 × Level
  • Additional armor steal per Way of Honor hero: 0.5% Physical Attack + 1 × Level

Battle Impact:

This skill provides both offensive and defensive utility, making Qing Mao a dual-threat hero. The armor-stealing effect not only amplifies her own damage but also makes enemies more vulnerable to allied physical attackers. The bonus armor steal from Way of Honor allies further enhances team synergy, creating opportunities for strategic compositions. Use this skill to counter high-armor teams, as it progressively weakens their defenses while boosting your team’s effectiveness throughout the fight.

Synergies with Honor Heroes

Qing Mao’s rework amplifies her synergy with other Way of Honor heroes, making her a key part of many compositions:

  • Tristan: His Armor penetration perfectly complements Qing Mao’s Armor-stealing, enabling relentless physical damage.
  • Soleil: Boosts team survivability and damage by redistributing stolen Armor and Magic defense.
  • Fafnir: Enhances crowd control with stuns and shields that sync well with Qing Mao’s Armor reduction.
  • Luther: Disrupts backlines, allowing Qing Mao to focus her damage output effectively.

Tank Slayer

Qing Mao's Talisman Guide

With Talisman, Qing Mao now receives 6000 critical chance points from her stats and 2000 agility points. Although critical chance alone is not enough, if Qing Mao is paired with Sebastian, Tristan, Oya, and other heroes who attack on the frontline, it will have a much more effective effect.

In terms of damage, the Talisman will affect her Ultimate, if Qing Mao significantly reduces enemy armor and hits enemies on the front line with critical damage with Sebastian present in her team, and armor penetration activated. Qing Mao will be able to destroy front-lines of entire enemies.

Analysis of Qing Mao's Talismans in Hero Wars Alliance

Qing Mao has two talismans that significantly enhance her combat effectiveness, each catering to distinct strategies: The Talisman of Loss and The Kinship Talisman.

Talisman of Loss

The Stone Talisman grants Agility and Critical Hit Chance, making Qing Mao a powerful damage dealer. The added Critical Damage Chance allows her to deal double damage, significantly amplifying her offensive potential.

Talisman of Loss Stats
Slot Stats Points
0 Agility +2000
1 Critical Chance +2200
2 Critical Chance +2200
3 Critical Chance +2200

This talisman is ideal for aggressive strategies, where maximizing her damage output is the priority. It pairs especially well with teams focused on high burst damage, as her critical hits can quickly eliminate key enemy heroes. However, this talisman may leave her vulnerable to counters from heroes like Helios, who specializes in punishing critical hits.

Kinship Talisman

The Kinship Talisman provides Health and Physical Attack, which improve her overall survivability and damage. Additionally, it enhances Qing Mao’s ability to reduce enemy armor, amplifying the damage dealt by her team, especially against high-armor opponents.

This talisman is more suitable for defensive or balanced strategies, as the increased health makes her more durable in the frontline, countering potential threats. It also protects her from counters like Helios or Amira, who thrive against critical-hit-focused teams.

Recommendation

For offense: Choose the Stone Talisman if you aim to maximize critical damage and deal devastating blows. Avoid using this talisman against teams with critical hit counters, such as Helios.

For defense: Opt for the Kinship Talisman when facing critical hit counters or if you want to improve Qing Mao’s survivability while still supporting your team’s armor-breaking capabilities.

How to Counter Qing Mao

Counters
 How enemies defeat Qing Mao

Ziri
Ziri is the character with the most Armor points in Hero Wars and when she uses her Ultimate ability she increases her physical and magical defense by 150%, so the armor reduction caused by Qing Mao is insignificant for Ziri.

Julius
Whenever a shield of Julius or an ally is destroyed, they gain a bonus to magical defense and armor, and all negative effects are eliminated. Thus, Julius becomes very strong against Qing Mao.

Celeste
Celeste can clear all negative effects applied to her allies, and when Qing Mao reduces the tank's armor. Celeste cleans and restores armor every time she heals an allied tank.
