Strategy on how to use Mushy and Shroom

Mushy and Shroom is a great hero with the ability to create clones of Melium and mushrooms that advance causing a lot of damage to heroes that fight on the front line.


  • Advertisement
     

For better use, it should be used with heroes that give a lot of healing to allies, like Martha and Celeste and even Morrigan.

As Melius and the mushrooms need health to activate, the new Hero Wars hero is definitely worth leveling up.

Positive and Negative Points

Positive Points

  • Resistant
  • Create Melium Clones and Mushrooms
  • Self Healing of Melio and Mushrooms
  • Silence enemies

Negative Points

  • Does not have abilities exclusive to nature heroes
  • Depends on high healing heroes to activate Melium and Mushrooms quickly

Stats Evolution Priority

Glyphs Priority

In Mushy and Shroom glyphs, prioritize health, magic attack, and intelligence to improve skills. Then level up, armor, and magical defense.

Priorities Glyphs
1st Health
2nd Magic Attack
3rd Intelligence
4th Armor
5th Magic Defense

Artifacts Priority

In Mushy and Shroom artifacts, prioritize the book artifact to gain more health and magical attack, which are the main statistics to increase the power of skills. Then level up the weapon to gain armor when using Ultimate and finally the ring to gain more magical attacks and magical defense.

Priorities Artifacts
1st Book
2nd Weapon (Armor)
3rd Ring

Skins Priority

In Mushy and Shroom skins, level up armor first, as she is a tank and needs defense on the front line. Then level up the Super Skin to increase the magical attacks in her skills. Lastly, level-up, intelligence, and magical defense.

Priorities Skins
1st Armor
2nd Super Skin Magic Attack
3rd Intelligence
4th Magic Defense



Mushy and Shroom vs Hydra

Mushy and Shroom are good heroes against Hydras and can deal a lot of damage with their abilities in combination with Aidan and Xe's ha. They are able to defeat the legendary Hydras with just one battle.

Max level Skills

1st Perfect Copy

Summons an inactive Melium with a portion of its health missing. The copy has the hero's stats from the start of the battle. Can be targeted by attacks, offensive spells and healing spells. When Melium's health is fully replenished for the first time, it activates. After that, he can attack and use all skills learned by the hero, but he will not be able to replenish energy. You cannot summon more than 5 Melians.
Initial Health: Max(165922) - (45% * life + (300 * Level )) Subsequent health: Max(288716) - (100% * Life) Key words: cannot be copied Timers and Distances: Animation Delay: 1.2

2nd Branched Mycelium

Conjures Mycelium with 3 dormant spore mushrooms with a portion of their health missing. They can be targeted by attacks, offensive spells, and healing spells. His maximum health is 30% of the hero's health at the start of the battle. Once the mushroom's health is fully replenished, it charges towards enemies and explodes, dealing damage and spreading a cloud of mushroom spores in a short range. The cloud silences those within range for 4.0 seconds. Nearby enemies unaffected by mushroom spores are still prioritized when targeting.
Initial Health: Max(28871) - (10% * Health) Explosion damage: Max(52345) - ((10% * Health) + (25% * Magic Attack) + (70 * Level)) Timers and Distances: Initial timeout: 3 Skill timeout: 12 Duration:4

3rd Rotten Seeds

Damages the nearest enemy. Damage is increased for each point of healing taken by allies since this ability was last activated.
Basic Damage: Max(58990) - (40% * Magic Attack) + (50 * (Level + 20)) + (10% * Life) Extra damage: (60% * Ally Healing) Timers and Distances: Skill timeout: 10 Duration:5 Animation delay: 1.5

4th Wild Growth

Inactive mushrooms and spore mushrooms can recover health by themselves. The effect stacks for each active mushroom on the battlefield.
Inactive Mushroom Health Recovery: Max(6499) - Else(1% * Health) + (4% * Magic Attack) + (10 * (Skill Level +40)) Timers and Distances: Skill timeout: 13 Duration:5 Animation delay: 0.88
