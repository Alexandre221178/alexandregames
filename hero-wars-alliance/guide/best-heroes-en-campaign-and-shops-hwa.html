Campaign Heroes: Where to Start

Once available, start farming her soul stones, as she‚Äôs an excellent damage dealer who works well in both PvE and PvP. Her strength grows steadily, making her a valuable addition to your team.

Astaroth: Your Go-To Tank

Role: Tank

  • Faction: Chaos
  • Why Invest: Astaroth is often the first tank you'll encounter in the campaign, and he‚Äôs worth every resource. Ginger: The Progress Hero

Role: Damage Dealer

  • Faction: Progress
  • Why Invest: Once considered a subpar choice, Ginger has seen a resurgence thanks to her utility in the Progress faction. With strong area-of-effect (AoE) damage, Ginger can help you clear campaign levels faster. Between Ginger and Keira, Ginger is often the better choice for new players focusing on the Progress faction.

Helios: The Critical Damage Counter

Role: Support

  • Faction: Honor
  • Why Invest: Helios is great for countering critical damage teams, making him a top pick when facing crit-heavy opponents. If you often encounter such teams, investing in Helios can provide strong defensive capabilities and improve your team‚Äôs survivability.

Role: Damage Dealer

  • Faction: Eternity
  • Why Invest: Keira is another viable early-game hero. Although Ginger might be more beneficial for a Progress-focused team, Keira is still strong, particularly when paired with heroes from the Eternity faction like Iris and Dante. If you lean towards an Eternity team, Keira is a good investment.

Phobos: The Mage Counter

Role: Control

  • Faction: Eternity
  • Why Invest: Phobos is an excellent counter to mage-heavy teams. His ability to control and disable enemy mages makes him a valuable asset in both campaign and PvP. Compared to Cornelius, Phobos is currently the stronger choice, especially in the Eternity faction.

Heidi: The Pure Damage Specialist

Role: Damage Dealer

  • Faction: Nature
  • Why Invest: Heidi is highly valued for his pure damage output, which bypasses dodge mechanics. This makes him a great counter to heroes like Dante, Octavia and teams built around dodge abilities. Heidi‚Äôs role as a pure damage dealer makes him indispensable in certain matchups.

Mojo: The Best for Campaign and Hydra

Role: Damage Dealer, Healer

  • Faction: Nature
  • Why Invest: Mojo is highly effective for Hydra battles when paired with Jhu. Even at just three stars, both can contribute greatly to your guild. As they level up, their damage increases, and they can eventually deal significant damage to even Legendary Hydras without needing to be fully maxed out.

Merchant: Where to Spend Your Currency

As you progress, you'll earn currency from various shops. Deciding where to spend this currency is crucial, as it can significantly impact your team's strength.

Town Shop: The Value of Dorian

Dorian:

  • Dorian's vampirism ability makes him a valuable support hero, even at low star levels. You don't need to max him out his utility lies in his ability to grant vampirism to allies, which continues to work even after he dies.

VIP Shop: The Value of Andvari and Sebastian

Andvari:

  • Avoid spending on Andvari in the VIP shop. While he's highly effective against K'arkh teams, there are other counters available. Additionally, with the introduction of the tank class, Andvari has lost his value as a makeshift tank due to being a warrior. He also can't defeat K'arkh teams alone and requires a second tank.

Sebastian:

  • A strong support hero, Sebastian is excellent at countering control teams. If you have access to him via the VIP shop, he‚Äôs a worthwhile investment.

Arena Shop: Prioritize Judge

  • If you‚Äôre building a Progress team, Judge is your top picks. He offer great synergy with other Progress heroes and provide excellent support output. Prioritize Judge at Shop and farm Ginger in campaign.

Extra Tip:

  • Invest in Astaroth as a tank for the Progress faction while you work on obtaining Julius. Julius is the best tank for this faction, but his soul stones are hard to come by.

Highwaymen Shop:

Faceless:

  • In the Highwaymen Shop, the best purchase is Faceless, a hero who fits into any team and has great synergy, blending well with both physical and mage teams, in addition to copying the enemy's ultimate abilities.
  • After that, you can choose between Lilith for the Chaos faction or Satori for the Mystery faction. Avoid Cornelius for now, as Phobos is a better alternative and can be found in the campaign.

Tower Shop: Consider Dante and Orion

Dante:

  • Dante is a highly flexible hero who excels in dodge-based teams. In 2024, Dante remains relevant and powerful, especially when paired with heroes like Octavia.

Orion:

  • With the arrival of Folio, Orion has seen a resurgence in popularity. If you‚Äôre looking to invest in a mage, Orion is a strong choice, particularly in synergy with Folio.

Outland Shop: Be Selective

Avoid Investing Too Early:

  • The Outland Shop offers various hero soul stones, but it's often better to wait for events where you can earn them more efficiently. However, if you must invest, prioritize Jorgen, Luther, and Qing Mao, as they offer solid utility. In this shop, it's also advisable to prioritize purchasing large skin stone .

Dungeon Shop: A Long-Term Investment

The Dungeon Shop provides valuable resources, but it‚Äôs easy to get overwhelmed by the options. Here‚Äôs how to navigate it:

  • The Grand Arena Shop is great, everyone. Rufus is a hero that doesn't need to be very strong to be helpful just getting him to Violet rank will make him effective enough.

Lian:

  • You can also focus on Lian, another hero who can provide good results even with low power.

  • While Jet is a formidable hero, farming his soul stones in the Soul Stones Shop is excessively expensive, especially if you‚Äôre a free-to-play (F2P) player or someone who spends minimally on the game. Currently, Jet isn‚Äôt performing well enough to justify the high cost, offering too little for the investment. As an F2P or low-spending player, avoiding Jet should be a priority.

  • Additionally, consider investing in EXP potions to quickly level up your heroes, providing a faster boost to your team‚Äôs overall power. Then, focus on purchasing orange equipment items to strengthen your main team.

Guild War Shop: Focus on Jhu and Nebula

Jhu:

  • Essential for dealing with Hydra bosses, Jhu should be your first purchase if you're focusing on Hydra battles.

Nebula:

  • A powerful support hero, Nebula can fit into almost any team, enhancing the damage output of your main damage dealer. She‚Äôs a must-have for any serious player.

Hydra Shop: Focus on Yasmine and Morrigan

As a beginner, managing your Hydra Coins can be tricky. Here's some advice:

Yasmine:

  • A top-tier assassin, Yasmine excels in critical damage teams. Investing in her can give you a powerful edge in both PvP and PvE content.

Morrigan:

  • If you're facing teams with resurrection abilities, Morrigan is invaluable. Her ability to prevent resurrections makes her a strategic choice against teams featuring Astaroth or Rufus.

Hero's Way Shop: Spend Wisely

Tristan and Xe'sha:

  • Tristan and Xe'sha are excellent heroes to purchase, but the scarcity of Hero's Way coins and the abundance of useful items available make them a difficult choice for beginners, as they must decide between resources or their soul stones.

Focus on Essential Purchases:

  • The Hero's Way Shop offers rare resources, but they come at a high cost. Prioritize upgrading your heroes' star levels and only purchase items that will provide immediate, significant benefits.
