Polaris is the newest hero in Hero Wars Alliance. She is a backline Control/Mage fighter from the Way of Progress faction, and her main stat is Intelligence. This guide will help you understand her abilities, talisman, synergies, and how to build her correctly even if you are a beginner.

Who Is Polaris?

Polaris is a star-powered being who sealed herself into a mortal body to avoid harming the world with her immense magic. She took refuge in the frozen lands of the Dominion. While she learns to control her power, she defends the Dominion using her icy abilities.

Polaris Role and Position

  • Role: Controller / Mage
  • Main Stat: Intelligence
  • Position: Backline
  • Faction: Way of Progress

Polaris Skills Explained

1. Pulsing Comet (Main Skill)

Effect: Stun + Damage over time

Polaris launches an ice comet behind the enemy team. It stuns all enemies for 6.0 seconds and deals damage every 0.5 seconds. The damage equals 15% of her Magic Attack + 2400.

Tip: The closer enemies are to the comet's landing spot, the longer they are stunned. This means Polaris is strongest when enemies are grouped up tightly.

2. Glacial Fortress

Effect: Energy gain + Area stun + Explosion damage

Polaris creates an ice obstacle that absorbs 3 hits. Every time it is attacked, Polaris gains 70 Energy. When destroyed, it explodes, dealing 20% of her Magic Attack + 3600 and stunning nearby enemies for 1 second.

Tip: This helps Polaris cast her main skill more often, because of the energy she gains.

3. Shooting Star

Effect: Area stun + Damage

Polaris throws a star shard into the center of the enemy team. All nearby enemies take 35% of her Magic Attack + 4200 and are stunned for 3 seconds.

Important synergy: For every ally from the Way of Progress faction (besides Polaris), the area of this skill becomes 25% larger.

4. Aurora Borealis (Passive Skill)

Effect: Magic damage amplification

When Polaris or any ally stuns an enemy who is already stunned, a Radiance effect is applied. This effect lasts for 8 seconds, increasing magic damage taken by that enemy.

Bonus: Allies from the Way of Progress deal even more damage to Radiant enemies.

Beginner Tips to Play Polaris Effectively

  • Use teams with lots of stuns. This makes her passive skill activate more often.
  • Prioritize Magical Crit Chance in Glyphs, Artifacts, and her Talisman to maximize burst damage.
  • Keep her protected. She is a backline hero with low defense, so pair her with tanks or healers.

Polaris vs. Control and Magic Teams

Polaris shines against teams with fast frontlines or tightly grouped enemies, as her multiple stuns and wide-area skills can completely disrupt their formation. However, she can struggle against specific counters. Satori, for example, punishes heroes who gain energy quickly and Polaris gains energy fast through her ice obstacle. Sebastian also limits her power by creating a shield that protects allies from control effects like stuns.

Additionally, heroes who reflect damage or are immune to control can reduce her impact. Helios is also a strong counter to Polaris, as he punishes both magical and physical critical hits, making her high magic crit build risky against him. Be cautious when using Polaris in these matchups, and consider team adjustments to handle those threats.

Why Polaris Is a Top-Tier Hero

With a perfect balance of stun control, magical burst, and team support, Polaris is not only powerful but also easy to use if built correctly. Her synergy with Way of Progress heroes makes her a core part of magic-based teams in the current meta.

Polaris Pros e Cons

Pros of Polaris

  1. Extremely strong area control

    Abilities with multiple area stuns (up to 6 seconds of stun with Pulsing Comet).

    Great for disrupting grouped teams and preventing counterattacks.

  2. Powerful synergy with the Way of Progress faction

    Enhances the area size of Shooting Star with same-faction allies.

    Increases magic damage with the Aurora Borealis passive, especially useful with Arachne, Judge, and Orion.

  3. High potential for explosive magic damage

    With high Magical Crit Chance, can deal devastating critical magic hits (2.5x damage).

    Scales well with artifacts, talisman, and glyphs.

  4. Indirect sustain with energy gain

    Gains energy when attacked thanks to Glacial Fortress, allowing for faster ultimate cycles.

âŒ Cons of Polaris

  1. High dependence on synergies

    Needs allies with control (stuns) to activate her full potential (Radiance passive).

    Performs much better in specific compositions than in generic lineups.

  2. Vulnerability to anti-control

    Sebastian can nullify her stuns, drastically reducing her utility.

    Heroes immune to control or who reflect damage also diminish her effectiveness.

  3. Fragile against certain magic counters

    Helios is a direct counter, punishing magic crits with his Solar Shield.

    She is also weak against Satori, who punishes her rapid energy gain with Fox Fire marks.

    Sebastian counters her control abilities by blocking stuns with his protective song, greatly reducing her effectiveness in team fights.
