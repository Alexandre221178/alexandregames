Gameplay Review of Throne and Liberty

A detailed review of the gameplay of Throne and Liberty for PC, PS5, and Xbox Series X|S. Explore its features, mechanics, and more.

Throne and Liberty Release Date

Released on October 1, 2024, Throne and Liberty is the latest MMORPG from NCSOFT, available for PC, PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X|S. The game invites players into the vast world of Solisium, offering cross-platform play so friends can join forces regardless of their chosen device. Below, I’ll share my detailed review of the gameplay and overall experience.

Cross-Platform Play and Multiplayer Features

One of the key highlights of Throne and Liberty is its cross-platform compatibility. Players on PC, Xbox Series X|S, and PlayStation 5 can seamlessly play together, fostering an inclusive gaming community. However, for those who prefer to play on a PS5-exclusive server, cross-platform play is disabled. This feature offers flexibility, but it’s important to note that there is no cross-progression, meaning progress made on one platform cannot be carried over to another.

The absence of cross-progression may be a drawback for players who switch between platforms, but the ability to play with friends across devices is a definite advantage, making it easy to team up for large-scale events and cooperative gameplay.

Combat and Customization

While the combat system in Throne and Liberty doesn’t reinvent the wheel, it offers enough variety to keep players engaged. The game uses a tab-target system typical of many MMORPGs, but with a hybrid twist that allows for more dynamic playstyles. Players can customize their builds extensively, choosing from different combinations of armor, gear, traits, and skills to match their preferred playstyle.

One of the standout features of the combat system is the upcoming update that will make it more customizable, adding skill enhancement options and introducing new weapons, such as the Spear. This update promises to inject more excitement and variety into the gameplay. For now, though, the current combat system is enjoyable, especially when experimenting with different weapon combinations. I found the Greatsword/Dagger combination particularly thrilling and had a blast unlocking all the available skills for this setup.

Visuals and Immersion

Throne and Liberty shines in terms of its visuals. The game world is richly detailed, featuring a range of environments from bustling cities to serene countrysides, forests, deserts, and beaches. The attention to detail in these landscapes enhances the player’s immersion, making exploration a rewarding experience. The game’s graphics quality is impressive, and even in heavily populated areas during large-scale events, the performance remains smooth without any stuttering, which is a technical achievement worth noting.

Another immersive element is the dynamic weather system, which affects both the environment and gameplay. For example, when it rains, mud puddles form, and during lightning storms, certain skills gain area-of-effect (AoE) enhancements. These environmental changes, including events like solar eclipses, add layers of depth and make the world feel alive and unpredictable.

Endgame Content and PvP

Once players reach the endgame, Throne and Liberty offers a variety of content to keep them engaged. There are open-world dungeons, dynamic events, world bosses, and solo or cooperative raids. A 24/7 PvP zone is also set to be introduced, offering large-scale PvP battles that will keep competitive players invested long after completing the main storyline. Siege warfare and castle sieges will also be added soon, providing even more PvP excitement for those who enjoy large-scale conflicts.

The gear progression system stands out as particularly engaging. Unlike some MMORPGs where switching builds requires starting from scratch, Throne and Liberty allows players to transfer their gear traits and skills to new gear. This means that your investment into building up your character never feels wasted, as your progress is carried forward even when you change playstyles or equipment.

Guild System and Solo Play

The game does a good job of catering to both group and solo players. Throne and Liberty encourages guild participation, offering rewards and bonuses that enhance the collaborative experience. However, solo players aren’t left out. The game provides ample content for solo adventurers, and even those who don’t actively participate in guild activities can still reap benefits from simply joining a guild.

This balance between solo and group content ensures that players of all preferences can find something rewarding in the game, whether they’re teaming up for massive PvP battles or quietly exploring the vast open world on their own.

Movement and Exploration

Exploration is one of the most enjoyable aspects of Throne and Liberty. Early on, players gain access to various movement options, such as gliding, morphing into different creatures, or swimming. These versatile movement mechanics make traveling around the world not just practical but fun and strategic. The transformation mechanic, where players can morph into animals to cross different terrains, adds another layer of depth to exploration.

Though I personally wish the game had more traditional mounts, the transformation system does its job well, adding both utility and enjoyment to exploration.

Quest Design and Events

The quests in Throne and Liberty are designed with care, avoiding the typical “kill X enemies” formula that plagues many MMORPGs. Instead, the quests offer engaging storylines and often feature cinematic cutscenes that add richness to the game’s lore. This storytelling approach, combined with dynamic, open-world events, makes each mission feel impactful and memorable.

Players will also encounter spontaneous events in the open world, encouraging cooperation and real-time interaction. These dynamic events add unpredictability and excitement to the game, keeping the world feeling alive and ever-changing.

Conclusion

Throne and Liberty brings together a beautifully crafted world, engaging combat, and a rich variety of content, making it a strong contender in the MMORPG genre. The game’s cross-platform play, vast open world, dynamic weather, and immersive quest design all contribute to a deeply engaging experience. While there are a few minor gripes, such as clunky dodge-roll mechanics and thick NPC outlines, these issues do little to detract from the overall enjoyment.

For a free-to-play game, Throne and Liberty delivers a next-gen experience while maintaining the nostalgic elements that long-time MMORPG fans will appreciate. Whether you’re exploring solo or battling alongside friends, there’s plenty to keep you coming back for more.

In short, if you’re a fan of MMORPGs, Throne and Liberty is definitely worth checking out and since it’s free, there’s no reason not to dive in!
